# User Research

## Primary User

Aspiring or early-career AI Product Manager preparing for competitive interviews.

## Jobs To Be Done

When I have an AI product idea, I want to turn it into a rigorous product strategy case so that I can demonstrate senior-level product thinking in interviews.

## User Segments

### AI PM Candidate

Pain points:

- Portfolio projects often look too technical or too shallow
- Hard to show product judgment beyond coding ability
- Needs a polished case study that demonstrates structured PM thinking

Success criteria:

- Can explain why the product matters
- Can defend AI architecture decisions
- Can show metrics, experiments, risks, and tradeoffs

### Founder / Builder

Pain points:

- Has many AI product ideas but weak prioritization
- Unsure whether to use RAG, agents, fine-tuning, or human review
- Needs a fast product strategy draft before building

Success criteria:

- Gets a realistic MVP scope
- Understands first target user
- Can validate before overbuilding

### Portfolio Reviewer

Pain points:

- Many projects feel like prompt wrappers
- Hard to see candidate's PM judgment
- Wants evidence of AI product sense and business thinking

Success criteria:

- Sees clear user, market, architecture, risk, and metric thinking
- Can evaluate the candidate's decision-making process
- Understands how the system creates business value

## Key Research Insights

- Users need structure more than open-ended chat.
- AI PM interviewers look for tradeoffs, not just generated documents.
- High-risk AI products need explicit human-in-loop and evaluation design.
- Portfolio projects need to show artifacts and reasoning, not just UI polish.
